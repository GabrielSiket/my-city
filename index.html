“To eat is a necessity, but to eat intelligently is an art.”

François de la Rochefoucauld

Here are 4 reasons why you deserve a clean, healthy lifestyle!

  1. Oral health: healthy teeth and gums

    • Teeth and gums need vitamins and minerals from the foods we eat to stay healthy and strong.
    • Processed foods not only do not contain beneficial nutrients, but also contain refined sugars that can damage your teeth.
    • Fruits, vegetables, quality (organic) meat and healthy fats are full of nutrients needed for a healthy smile.
    • Did you know that chewing raw fruits and vegetables can strengthen our teeth, moreover, this process increases the production of saliva that washes away bad bacteria and removes them.
    • Many natural foods, such as blueberries, are natural antimicrobials.
    • They can kill bacteria, which can cause gum injury and even gingivitis.

  2. Perfect digestion

    • A healthy digestive system is the secret to a healthy body. If we do not eat healthy, then we may feel bloated, bloated and tired.
    • "Clean" foods are easily recognized by the body so that the body knows how to use the nutrients in what we eat!
    • Whole grains, nuts, seeds, fruits and vegetables are also high in fiber.
    • High-fiber foods also help maintain digestive health, which helps maintain a normal weight.
    • Processed foods, such as chips and pastries, are low in fiber and can cause inflammation in the gut.

  3. Improving mental health

    • Eating clean and healthy helps you to have a healthy mind.
    • About 30% of the body's energy is used by our brain.
    • So the healthier we eat, the more vitamins, nutrients, and energy we have, so our brains work better.
    • An omega-3 fatty acid deficiency can cause mood swings and even depression.
    • So by including enough sources of omega-3 in your diet, such as flax seeds, nuts, salmon and green leaves, you can prevent such a "fashionable" disease nowadays, DEPRESSION.
    • Vitamin B-6 also helps you keep your memory, and it is found in foods such as bananas, organic turkey, sweet potatoes, green leaves and sunflower seeds.

  4. Lower risk for cardiovascular disease

    • Alcohol, refined sugar and vegetable oils all cause chaos in our blood vessels. Repeated use of these foods can lead to high blood pressure and high cholesterol and possibly cardiovascular disease.
    • But a healthy diet can help maintain healthy circulation.
    • Low-fat or high-fat foods such as Brazil nuts, almonds, green tea, blueberries, oatmeal and tomatoes benefit the body and maintain cardiovascular health.
    • From minerals to antioxidants, they help keep your blood vessels clean and healthy.
